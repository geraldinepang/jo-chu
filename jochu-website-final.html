The Philosophy

What lies between
sake and
the infinite

JOCHU was born from a singular question: what if the full spectrum of sake's beauty — its floral grace, mineral depth, and silken texture — could be elevated to the strength and complexity of a fine spirit?

Naorai's answer is a proprietary low-temperature purification process that has no precedent in the spirits world. The result is something entirely new.

"We did not set out to make a better spirit. We set out to make something that had never existed."
01
Premium Junmai Sake
Only the finest junmai sake — pure rice wine with no added alcohol — forms the base of every batch.
02
Cold Distillation at <40°C
Proprietary vacuum distillation below 40°C preserves volatile aromatics lost in conventional heat-based processes.
03
Purification & Refinement
Multiple purification stages remove harsh congeners while retaining JOCHU's unmistakable signature — clean, layered, alive.

The Category
New Classification

JOCHU sits in no existing regulatory category. It is not sake, not shochu, not whisky. This novelty is its greatest commercial opportunity — and its most compelling story for sommelier and buyer alike.

About

Naorai Inc.

Origin

Founded in Hiroshima Prefecture, Naorai Inc. draws on the region's centuries-old sake brewing tradition while pushing into entirely uncharted territory. Hiroshima's soft water and premium rice have long made it one of Japan's most celebrated sake regions.

Innovation

The proprietary cold-distillation technology behind JOCHU took years to develop and refine. It represents a genuine technological breakthrough — a new way to think about what distillation can and cannot do to a spirit's character.

Global Vision

With US distribution established via Kierumi and European expansion underway, Naorai is executing a deliberate, quality-first global strategy. JOCHU is not positioned for volume — it is positioned for permanence in the premium spirits canon.
